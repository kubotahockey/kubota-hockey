About Us

KUBOTA Hockey seeks to provide high quality projections for professional hockey players around the world.

Having enjoyed success with the initial release of the KUBOTA projection system in 2023-2024, the new website for KUBOTA hockey has taken things even further. With improvements made to the base model along with an all new NHLe model, KUBOTA hockey is your one stop shop for fantasy and hockey enthusiasts alike.

Based on the PECOTA system developed by Nate Silver in the early 2000’s, KUBOTA uses k-nearest neighbor machine learning to determine historical comparables for almost every rostered and NHL affiliated player heading into the current season.

This allows for an age regression to occur for a specific player tailored to players from the past who profile most similarly. 37 year old Sidney Crosby will not regress the same way your average 37 year old would. This is the crux of the KUBOTA system.

The Nitty Gritty

KUBOTA Hockey currently consists of three unique player models to provide a wide range of predictions that best accommodate the players within our database.

1. KUBOTA NHL
- The most comprehensive of the three and Kubota Hockey’s core model.
- Finds player comparables dating back to the 1963 season.
- Projects the following stats for all NHL players who have played NHL games in all three of the prior seasons:

  • GP (Games Played)
  • G (Goals)
  • A (Assists)
  • PTS (Points)
  • SOG (Shots on Goal)
  • PIM (Penalty Minutes)
  • +/- (Plus/Minus)
  • PPG (Power Play Goals)
  • PPA (Power Play Assists)
  • SHG (Short-Handed Goals)
  • SHA (Short-Handed Assists)

2. KUBOTA NHL PERIPHERALS
- Due to NHL hit and block data being made available after the 2007 season, this model performs the same process as the original KUBOTA NHL model but specifically for HITS and BLOCKS:

  • BLK (Blocks)
  • HIT (Hits)

3. KUBOTA NHL EQUIVALENCIES
- The most ambitious of the three, the NHL Equivalencies model attempts to project how all NHL-affiliated players with fewer than 3 seasons of NHL experience would perform in the NHL this season, given their production in lower-level leagues.
- Due to limited data available, the model provides the following statistics:

  • GP (Games Played)
  • G (Goals)
  • A (Assists)
  • PTS (Points)
  • PIM (Penalty Minutes)
